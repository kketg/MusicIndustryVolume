But what about streaming?

Since streaming isn't measured in individual sales like the other formats, and a song isn't duplicated when someone listens to it on stremaing, we only need to account for every song available on streaming once.


There are ~184,000,000 songs available for streaming**


So, just for fun, lets figure out how how small of a space we can fit all of the world's music.
If you wanted to store 184,000,000 songs in as little space as possible, it could fit in...

~1,610 M.2 2230 SSDs
~3220 Terabytes*

Which would only take up ~0.0024 cubic meters!

† All volume data and visual representations of physical objects is purely approximation based on the standard dimensions of physical formats and data size averages of commercial music. They are not exactly to scale.

* As of April 2024, the largest commercially available consumer hard drive has a storage size of 30 Terabytes. The largest commercially available consumer M.2 2230 SSD is 2 Terabytes.

** In their 2023 Year End Report on the music industry, Luminate tracked ~184 million ISRCs on streaming platforms, each one representing an individual song.
